School Overview

Assumption College of Davao (previously Assumption School of Davao) is a private Catholic higher educational institution in Davao City. It was established in 1958 by the Daughters of Mary the Assumption (FMA). Initially named Assumption Academy of Davao, the school was built to have a city-based branch of the first Assumption School founded by the FMA sisters. The academy offered primary and secondary programs exclusively for girls. In 1961, the college department opened for enrollment, and three years later the administration accepted elementary school boys. In 1967, the school offered kindergarten classes; and, eventually it also became co-educational. At present, the institution is run by the Missionaries of the Assumption..
